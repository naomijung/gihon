our mission
Our mission is to empower the women of Kitengela by providing them with the skills and confidence they need to reach their full potential and give them a voice that can be heard.
our history
Gihon Women Empowerment Kenya was founded in 2015 by Agnes Gitonga and Lucy Wanjin in Kitengela, Kenya. Agnes initially set up a feeding program to give the children in the local area a chance to come and receive a free meal, but when she noticed that many women were also queuing up to be fed, she realised that something had to be done. Lucy and Agnes set out to create a one-year annual empowerment program that would improve the lives of the women in Kitengela by helping them bring in their own income.

Gihon is a non-government funded organisation which focuses on gender-equality and empowering women through both formal lessons and practical skills. The women who take part in the program are able to learn a wide range of new skills that will give them the tools they need to succeed and achieve their dreams, all free of charge.

As the center has grown over the years, Gihon has initiated other projects as well, including a school program for underprivileged kids in Kitengela and a support group for HIV positive women. Read more about our work here!
